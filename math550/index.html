Course objectives/learning outcomes:

Successful students will:
  • Review, solidify, and build upon concepts first learned in Math 241 (Multivariable Calculus).
  • Understand the geometry of vectors, vector fields, and vector-valued functions in Euclidean space.
  • Master extensions of the differentiation and integration operations to multivariate and vector-valued functions.
  • Master the perspective of differentiation as a linear map on tangent spaces.
  • Understand the multivariate generalizations of the Fundamental Theorems of Calculus: theorems of Green, Gauss, and Stokes -- together with the "Generalized Stokes Theorem".
  • Learn the basics of differential geometry: manifolds, differential forms, curvature, and the like.
  • Understand applications of all of the above to physics, including Kepler's laws and the Maxwell equations.
  • Continue developing fluency in abstract, rigorous mathematics -- preparing the student for study of more advanced topics.
