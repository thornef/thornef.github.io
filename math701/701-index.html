Course objectives and learning outcomes:

In Math 701 and 702, successful students will:

  • Master algebraic topics concerning groups, rings, fields, and other algebraic structures.

  • More importantly, develop their sense of algebraic thinking, so that they can more easily absorb additional algebraic material in the future.

  • Lay the foundation for research in topics including algebraic number theory, algebraic combinatorics, representation theory, algebraic geometry, commutative algebra, algebraic topology, etc., etc., etc.

  • Prepare for the Qualifying Exam in algebra.

Course Textbooks:

In large part I will closely be following Dummit and Foote's Abstract Algebra. I encourage to obtain a copy of the book and follow along.

There are a number of other excellent books on algebra as well. Perhaps the most interesting is Lang's Algebra. In my opinion Lang's book is not very good for the beginner, but is excellent for someone who has seen the material before and wants to review or see a different perspective. In particular, it is great to read when you are studying for quals.

Course Requirements:

  • (70%) Written homework assignments will be assigned at most weekly. Collaboration is strongly encouraged.

  • (30%) There will be a final exam and one or more midterm exams, with questions similar to what you may expect on the qualifying exams. All exams will be take-home, pledged, closed book, and of at least three hours in duration.
  • (10% -- Bonus) Regularly attend the graduate colloquium, and/or the department colloquium or seminars in your mathematical area of interest.

Grading Scale:

(Shamelessly stolen from Matt Ballard.)

  • A: You have a strong chance of passing the qualifying exam if you prepare reasonably. This also represents a strong foundation for learning further related topics.
  • B+: With additional effort, you should be able to pass the qualifying exam if you prepare. This represents a partial foundation for learning further related topics.
  • B: You have demonstrated some mastery of the subject material, but should put in a lot of additional effort if you want to pass the qualifying exam or write a thesis in a related area.
  • < B: This indicates a serious problem.
