The Columbia Math Circle

Welcome!

The Columbia Math Circle is an enrichment activity for high school students in the Columbia, SC Area.

What Is A Math Circle?

Math Circles are communities focused on the enjoyment of mathematical problem solving. Meetings are lively, interactive, challenging, and fun.

Much of the discussion will be open ended: often the questions will be vague, and it will be up to you to figure out how to make them precise -- or to think of your own questions to ask and answer!

Ours is one of many Math Circles throughout the nation!

What Isn't A Math Circle?

Each Math Circle will focus on a single advanced topic -- often not something covered in high school. If you're looking for extra help for your classes, or SAT tutoring, then this isn't really the place.

Also the Math Circle is not competition-focused. For many, competitions are great fun! And preparing for them can be a great way to learn a lot of math.

First Meeting

The first meeting will be Saturday, March 2 from 10:00-11:30 am at the Hammond School (see below for directions).

The topic will be Probability and The Price Is Right. We will look at some clips from The Price Is Right, and try to figure out the contestants' probabilities of winning. Some of these questions are easy, and some are very difficult.

Future meetings will cover the gamut of math -- algebra, combinatorics, geometry, and much else!

Instructors

Frank Thorne, Director and Head Instructor

Frank Thorne is Professor of Mathematics at the University of South Carolina. His research interests include analytic number theory and arithmetic statistics, and he has spoken on his research all over the world. He has taught math courses at all levels of the college curriculum, and developed a unique course on the mathematics of game shows, introducing topics such as discrete probability and modeling via popular shows like The Price Is Right.

In his free time, Frank enjoys hiking, yoga, martial arts, and piano.

What's required?

Please bring a pen/pencil and some paper. You will not need a calculator or a computer, although you might find a use for them if you bring them.

Most of all, please bring your curiosity! Mathematics is about exploration and discovery.

There is currently no cost for attendance. But please register before attending the meeting.
